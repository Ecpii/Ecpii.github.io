Multiplayer

How is a puzzle game turned into a multiplayer experience? How do players interact?

In singleplayer, much of the goal in Tetris is to earn the most points by clearing lines and placing blocks. In multiplayer, the goal is a bit different - to top out your opponent.

Sending Garbage

In order to top out your opponent, you send them garbage when you clear lines. How much garbage you send depends on how many lines you clear at once, the type of clear, and other factors. It's all detailed in the following table:

T-Spins

In the garbage table, you may have noticed the word "T-Spin". These refers to line clears done by soft-dropping the T-piece into a slot and rotating it. Of course, that is an over-simplified description, and more details can be found here.

The most important part about T-Spins, though, are their efficiency. In the garbage table, you will notice that T-Spin Doubles will send the same amount of garbage as Quads. However, T-Spin Doubles only requires you to clear two lines, while Quads require you to clear four lines. This means that you can build less (and spend less time stacking) to send the same amount of garbage. This is why they're so often used in modern Tetris.

Back 2 Back

In the garbage table above, you may have also noticed the acronym "B2B". This refers to consecutively clearing only Quads or T-Spins. As you do so, you send more and more garbage with each attack. However, the B2B chain can be easily broken by just one skim.

Receiving Garbage

When your opponent sends you garbage, it gets stored in a queue on the side of your board. It will not be sent to your board immediately, but only after you place a piece which does not clear a line.

If you do place a piece that clears a line, the garbage will not be transferred to your board - it will, however, remain in your queue. If the line clear you just performed was able to send garbage, the amount you sent will be subtracted from the amount of garbage you are waiting to receive. This allow you to cancel attacks with other attacks.
